# Business Problem Statement

Enterprise AI programs often stall because they begin with isolated pilots instead of modernizing the underlying business and technology capabilities. Teams build impressive demos, but struggle to scale them into reliable products because data quality, integration, security, and ownership issues remain unresolved.

## Common Symptoms

- AI pilots depend on manually extracted data.
- Business users do not trust model outputs.
- Source systems are integrated through brittle point-to-point interfaces.
- Data products do not have clear ownership or quality contracts.
- Security teams cannot audit prompts, retrieval context, or model responses.
- Platform teams duplicate infrastructure for each AI initiative.
- ROI is discussed but not connected to operational metrics.

## Root Causes

- Legacy systems were not designed for real-time data access.
- Enterprise data is fragmented across applications and document stores.
- AI access controls are bolted on late.
- Teams lack a shared reference architecture.
- Governance is treated as a review gate instead of an embedded capability.

## Desired Future State

The organization should operate an AI-ready modernization platform where trusted data products, event streams, model gateways, vector indexes, audit trails, and reusable deployment patterns are available to product teams by default.
